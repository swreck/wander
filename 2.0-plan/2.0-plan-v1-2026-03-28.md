# Wander 2.0 Build Plan

## What This Document Is

This is the complete build plan for Wander 2.0. Once approved, I'll execute the whole thing on a git branch, stopping only for genuine decisions that need your judgment. When complete, you'll review the result as a whole experience, not phase by phase.

Everything here flows from our discussion: the three-user model (Architect / Co-Planner / Passenger), Scout as local friend, freedom over constraint, comfortably optional input, and the shift from planning tool to trip companion.

---

## What 2.0 Is NOT

- Not a rewrite. The backend, data model, and most infrastructure stay. This is a UX transformation on top of solid plumbing.
- Not social. No public profiles, no sharing beyond the trip group.
- Not gamified. No badges, streaks, or engagement metrics.
- Not Scout-as-boss. Every suggestion dismissable. Every flow completable without Scout.

---

Before any 2.0 code:
- Create `2.0` branch from current `main`
- All work happens there
- `main` remains untouched — instant rollback if needed

## Build 1: The Day Timeline

**Why first:** This is the single change that most transforms how Wander feels. It replaces the database-viewer day page with something that reads like a day in your life.

**What changes:**

The current day view (a list of promoted experiences, then possible experiences, then reservations, then accommodations) becomes a visual timeline divided into morning / afternoon / evening sections.

- **Committed items** (reservations with confirmed times) show as solid cards with time badges
- **Planned items** (promoted experiences with time windows) show as regular cards in their section
- **Free time** appears as a gentle indicator between items — not empty space, but a visible "open" slot
- **Possible experiences are removed from the day view entirely.** They belong to the city, not the day. This is the biggest conceptual shift: a day shows what's happening, not what might happen.

For the Architect: tapping a free-time slot opens a quick-add that shows nearby saved experiences from the city, or lets you ask Scout.

For the Passenger: just read top to bottom. Morning, afternoon, evening. Done.

**Technical scope:**
- New `DayTimeline.tsx` component replacing the current day content in `PlanPage.tsx`
- Existing data model unchanged — experiences still have `timeWindow` (morning/afternoon/evening) and `dayId`
- Reservation times map to timeline position
- Accommodation check-in/check-out shown as bookend cards

**What I won't touch:** The map, the bottom nav, the drawer system, Scout chat. Just the day content area.

## Build 2: City Idea Boards

**Why second:** Once days show only what's planned, the city needs to be where you browse, discover, and express interest. This is also where group collaboration happens naturally.

**What changes:**

Each city gets its own page — an idea board. Currently you see a city's experiences through filtered lists. Now you see them as a visual board grouped by theme.

**Layout:**
- **Header:** City photo (Cloudinary), date range (or "X days" for dateless), accommodation name and address
- **Idea cards grouped by theme:** Food, temples, nature, shopping, art, nightlife, etc. Each card shows: name, who saved it, rating if available, and a subtle indicator if it's already slotted into a day
- **Interest signals:** A heart on any card means "I want to do this." Tap once to add, tap again to remove. Completely optional. No empty-state shaming. The Architect sees aggregate counts ("3 people") but never sees who DIDN'T react. Non-participation is invisible.
- **Bottom section:** "Add an idea" (opens capture) and "Ask Scout about this city" (opens chat with city context)

**For the Architect:** The board shows which ideas have energy. "4 people hearted the cooking class, 0 hearted the war museum." This is signal for day-building without requiring formal votes.

**For the Passenger:** Browse, heart things that look fun, add your own finds. No understanding of the planning system required.

**Technical scope:**
- New `CityBoard.tsx` page component
- New `IdeaCard.tsx` component with heart interaction
- New `ExperienceInterest` model in Prisma (travelerId, experienceId, type: "heart") — lightweight, not the existing Decision/Vote system
- API endpoint for interest signals (POST/DELETE)
- Scout tool: `get_city_interests` to see what people are excited about
- Navigation: tapping a city name on the dashboard opens the city board instead of filtering the day list

**Relationship to existing features:** The existing Decision/Vote system stays for formal group choices. Hearts are informal, always-on, no open/close sessions. Different purpose.

## Build 3: Phase-Aware Dashboard

**Why third:** The dashboard orchestrates everything. It needs the timeline and city boards to exist so it can link to them meaningfully.

**What changes:**

The TripOverview home screen becomes context-aware. It detects which phase the trip is in and shows the right thing.

**Pre-planning (dates TBD):**
- Trip name, member avatars
- Cities as reorderable cards: "First stop → Second stop → Third stop"
- Per-city: idea count, heart count, accommodation status
- Prominent "Add ideas" and "Invite people" actions
- Scout nudge area (one suggestion, dismissable): "You have 3 cities but no restaurants saved yet."

**Planning (dates set, 2+ weeks out):**
- Calendar grid (existing, refined) where each day shows a "vibe" — dominant theme emoji + short label ("Temple day", "Food tour + free afternoon")
- Unplanned days have a soft glow or subtle indicator
- Planning progress: "18 of 24 days have activities"
- Coverage gaps visible: "Ho Chi Minh City has 3 activities across 4 days"
- Approval badge (existing) + new "X people interested in Y" summary

**Approaching (under 2 weeks out):**
- Same as planning, plus: weather preview, document readiness ("2 people haven't shared passport info"), packing hints based on scheduled activities ("you have 2 outdoor hikes — check rain gear")

**During trip:**
- Today's timeline front and center (the Build 1 timeline, embedded)
- Tomorrow as a compact preview below
- Active alerts: weather, transit disruptions, "leave in 20 minutes"

**Post-trip:**
- Trip summary card: cities, days, activities completed, contributors
- "Create a trip memory" prompt (links to Build 7)
- "Start planning your next trip"

**Technical scope:**
- Phase detection logic: compare trip dates to current date, check `datesKnown`
- Refactor `TripOverview.tsx` into phase-specific sub-components
- Calendar day cells get "vibe" computation (dominant theme of scheduled activities)
- New `PlanningProgress.tsx` component
- Scout nudge component (reusable for later builds)

**What stays:** The map, the bottom nav, trip switching, the existing approval queue and learnings panels.

## Build 4: Now Screen — Before, During, After

**Why fourth:** With the dashboard and timeline working, Now becomes the "what needs my attention" layer.

**What changes:**

**Before trip (planning phase):**
Now shows planning awareness — what's missing, what needs attention, what might be a problem.

- Coverage gaps: "Hanoi has 8 activities but no restaurants. Day 4 has nothing scheduled."
- Group signals: "The cooking class has 5 hearts — might be worth scheduling."
- Spatial insights: "Your hotel is in the Old Quarter. 4 saved spots are walking distance, but 3 are across the river."
- Density warnings: "December 27 has 6 activities. That's ambitious — the temple and market alone are a half-day."
- Document/readiness status as trip approaches

Each insight is a card. Dismissable. Actionable (tap to go to the relevant day or city). Not a task list — more like a friend saying "hey, did you think about this?"

**A soft preview of during-trip Now:** At the bottom of pre-trip Now, a small section: "During your trip, Now will show your live timeline, walking distances, and leave-time alerts. It'll look something like this:" with a compact mockup using the first planned day's data.

**During trip:**
- Today's timeline (same as dashboard, but here it's the full focus — no calendar grid, no city overview, just today)
- "What's next" with distance and leave-time
- GPS dot on map showing your position relative to next destination
- Adaptive suggestions when plans change ("It's raining — the museum is 8 minutes away")
- Morning brief card (appears once per morning, dismissable): weather, today's plan summary, any alerts

**After trip:**
- Trip stats: days, cities, activities completed, kilometers walked (if GPS data available)
- Learnings captured
- Link to trip memory creation

**Technical scope:**
- Refactor `NowPage.tsx` to be phase-aware (same logic as dashboard)
- New `PlanningInsight.tsx` component for pre-trip cards
- Insight generation: backend endpoint that analyzes trip structure and returns actionable observations
- Morning brief: scheduled card that appears based on time of day during trip dates
- The during-trip Now is largely existing code, reorganized and enhanced

## Build 5: Scout Everywhere

**Why fifth:** The surfaces exist (timeline, city board, dashboard, Now). Now Scout can appear contextually on each of them.

**What changes:**

Scout moves from "a chat panel you open" to "a presence that appears when relevant." The chat panel still exists for open-ended conversation. But most Scout interactions happen in-context.

**Contextual nudges (small, dismissable bubbles):**
- On the day timeline, at a free-time slot: "There are 3 saved restaurants near your morning temple. Want one for lunch?"
- On the city board, when a theme has many ideas: "You have 6 food spots saved for Hanoi. Want me to help pick the best 3?"
- On the dashboard, when a pattern emerges: "Days 3-5 are packed but Day 6 is empty. Want to spread things out?"
- On Now, when conditions change: "Your afternoon hike might get rained out. The ceramics studio is nearby and indoors."

**Inline actions:**
- Long-press any experience card → "Ask Scout about this" opens chat pre-filled with context
- On the city board: "Scout, what should we do in Hanoi?" button that opens chat with city context injected

**Proactive learning surfacing:**
- When viewing a city that has relevant learnings from past trips, a small card appears: "From Japan 2026: big groups struggled at formal restaurants. Still want to add this?"
- Dismissable. Never blocking.

**The key constraint:** Every nudge can be dismissed with one gesture. No nudge repeats after dismissal. No "Scout thinks you should..." — always "in case it's useful" framing. Scout never blocks an action or requires interaction before proceeding.

**Technical scope:**
- New `ScoutNudge.tsx` component (reusable across all surfaces)
- Backend: new endpoint `POST /api/scout/suggestions` that takes context (current view, trip state, time) and returns 0-2 suggestions
- Suggestion generation uses Claude with trip context (similar to existing chat system prompt, but targeted)
- Nudge dismissal stored in localStorage (not server — lightweight, private)
- Existing chat panel unchanged — these are additions, not replacements

## Build 6: Collaboration — Feed, Reactions, Group Visibility

**Why sixth:** With the city boards and interest signals from Build 2 in place, this build adds the social layer that makes group planning feel alive.

**What changes:**

**Activity feed:**
A lightweight stream of what's happening in the trip. Not push notifications — a feed you can glance at.

- "Larisa added Café Pho Co to Hanoi"
- "Brian hearted the cooking class"
- "Ken scheduled Day 3: Old Quarter walking tour, Bun Cha for lunch"
- "Ava joined the trip"

Accessible from the dashboard (small "recent activity" section) and as a full page.

**Reactions on activities (beyond hearts):**
Hearts (from Build 2) are the primary signal. But on the city board, each idea card also supports:
- A small comment/note: "My friend said this place is incredible" — attributed, visible to the group
- These are NOT formal reviews. Just quick thoughts. Like texting the group chat about a place.

**Group preference visibility (Architect view):**
On the city board, the Architect sees a subtle overlay: which ideas have the most hearts, which have comments, which have zero engagement. This is the signal layer for day-building.

On a day being built, if the Architect adds something with 0 hearts and skips something with 5 hearts, no warning — just information. Freedom, not constraint.

**Technical scope:**
- New `ActivityFeed` model in Prisma (auto-generated from existing ChangeLog data + new interest events)
- `ActivityFeed.tsx` component
- Comment/note on experiences: small `ExperienceNote` model (travelerId, experienceId, content)
- API endpoints for feed and notes
- Scout tool: `get_trip_activity` to see recent group actions

## Build 7: Reflections and Trip Memory

**Why seventh:** The trip lifecycle features. These make Wander valuable after the trip ends, not just during planning.

**What changes:**

**End-of-day reflection (during trip):**
A soft card appears in the evening (after 6pm local time, during trip dates). "How was today?"

- Tap a heart on any activity from today to mark it as a highlight
- Drop a photo (standard file picker — works in Safari PWA)
- Say a word to Scout ("the cooking class was incredible") — saved as a learning automatically
- Swipe away to dismiss. No guilt. No "you haven't reflected on 3 days."
- If dismissed, never mentioned again for that day.

**End-of-trip summary:**
After the trip end date, a card on the dashboard: "Welcome home. Want to see your trip story?"

The trip story is a **scrollable, interactive narrative** — not a slideshow, not a PDF.

- City by city, day by day
- Photos matched to activities (manually added via reflections, or bulk-added post-trip via file picker with date filtering)
- Learnings woven in naturally: "Day 3 taught you: book restaurants 2 weeks ahead for groups over 6"
- Contributor attribution: "Larisa found Café Pho Co, which became the group's morning spot"
- Activity highlights (the hearts from daily reflections) featured prominently
- Simple stats card: "8 days, 2 cities, 47 activities, 12 restaurants, 3 temples"
- Map trace showing the route through each city

**Two versions:**
- **Personal:** Your photos, your highlights, activities you attended (respects split-day subgroups). The one you look at on a quiet evening.
- **Group:** Even coverage of people and places. Shared highlights. The one you send to the family group chat. Shareable as a single link (a beautiful web page).

**Photo integration (PWA constraints):**
- During trip: daily reflection card offers "add a photo" via standard picker
- Post-trip: "I'd like to add photos from your camera roll. Your trip ran from Dec 25 to Jan 1. Pick photos from that period and I'll match them to your days." Multi-select file picker, Wander matches by date/time to the itinerary, user confirms placement.
- Future (Capacitor native): automatic camera roll scanning by date range with permission

**Customization:**
Each card in the trip story is editable:
- Reorder by dragging
- Remove with a swipe
- Add a note or caption
- Expand or collapse sections
- The story is a living document, not a rendered artifact

**Technical scope:**
- New `Reflection` model (dayId, travelerId, highlights: string[], photoUrl?, note?)
- New `TripStory` model (tripId, type: "personal" | "group", sections: JSON, published: boolean)
- `ReflectionCard.tsx` — the evening prompt component
- `TripStory.tsx` — the interactive narrative viewer/editor
- `TripStoryGenerator` service — takes trip data, reflections, photos, and generates the narrative structure
- Photo upload to Cloudinary (existing infrastructure)
- Shareable story page: public URL with read-only view
- Scout tools: `create_trip_story`, `add_reflection`

## Build 8: Onboarding — The First 60 Seconds

**Why eighth:** With all the new surfaces built, onboarding can properly introduce them.

**What changes:**

When someone taps an invite link for the first time, they don't land on a feature tour. They land in context.

**Step 1: "Here's what's been planned"**
A visual summary of the trip — not raw data, but a narrative card.
"The Fong Family Vietnam trip: 8 days across Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City. 15 activities planned so far, from street food tours to temple visits. Larisa added a cooking class yesterday that 4 people are excited about."

**Step 2: "A few things about you"**
Not a form. A short Scout conversation (3-4 questions max):
- "What kind of experiences do you love?" (tappable interest tags, same as current, but framed conversationally)
- "Anything you definitely want to do in Vietnam?" (free text → Scout saves as an experience idea)
- "Early bird or night owl?" (single tap)
Each question is skippable. "Skip — you can always tell Scout later."

**Step 3: Land on the dashboard with a Scout message**
"Based on what you told me, you might especially like the cooking class and the Cu Chi Tunnels. Take a look?" Links directly to those items on the city board.

**For return visitors:** No onboarding replay. Just the dashboard. If they haven't set preferences, Scout might gently surface one question in a nudge: "Quick question — are you a morning person? It'll help me plan better days for you."

**Technical scope:**
- Refactor `NewMemberOnboarding.tsx` to use the three-step flow
- Trip summary generation (backend: `GET /api/trips/:id/summary` returns narrative text)
- Scout conversational onboarding (3-4 scripted questions with free-text follow-up)
- Preference-to-recommendation matching in onboarding step 3

## Build 9: Capture Simplification

**Why ninth:** Polish. The capture system works but has five entry points. Simplify to one.

**What changes:**

A single floating action button (FAB) on every screen:
- **Tap:** Opens camera
- **Long-press:** Shows three options — Paste text, Enter URL, Voice ("Say what you found")
- Voice capture: "Save Bun Cha Huong Lien, it's Obama's pho place in Hanoi" → Scout extracts name, associates with Hanoi, enriches. Confirmation only if something's ambiguous.

The existing capture infrastructure (AI extraction pipeline) stays. This is just the entry point simplification.

**Technical scope:**
- New `CaptureFAB.tsx` component (replaces current scattered entry points)
- Voice capture integration (existing Web Speech API infrastructure)
- Context-aware: FAB knows which city you're viewing and pre-fills context
- Remove redundant capture buttons from other surfaces (the FAB is the universal entry)
